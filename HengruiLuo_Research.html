=====Seminars=====

Summer 2020
Fall 2020		 Non-Smoothness In Quantum Field Models
This is a collection of notes and research readings before and during my first project at LBNL. Rather than the classical $L^2$ integrable wave functions discussed in most quantum mechanic literature, we explore some classes of locally modified non-smooth wave functions; multi-modal wave functions and compactly supported wave functions. The motivation is from quantum mechanics and the quantum field theory. However, the approach we took here is through computer-assisted simulation within statistical surrogate framework. No public access.
Autumn 2019* Coupling And Stochastic Calculus
This is a collection of notes I took when reading on the coupling method in classic probability theory, with some extensions. The extensions focus on the intrinsic connection between successful couplings and the convergence of Markov diffusion processes, which is a central topic in stochastic calculus. At the same time I did parallel readings for the "STAT8810. Stochastic Calculus" course given during the Fall 2019 by Prof. Radu Herbei.

Autumn 2019		 Inequalities And Bounds Of Stochastic Processes
This is mainly about discussions, practices and learning from Prof.Talagrand. No public access.
Summer 2019* Pseudo-Convexity In Optimizations
This is mainly a collection of readings that I went through with my friend Renxiong Liu at OSU. In our meetings, we discussed the numerical optimization algorithms as well as optimization oriented statistical methods. Theoretically I spent some time in the  multivariate complex analysis and p-adic analysis. For the line in multivariate complex analysis I end up in reading some modern applications of the Bergman reproducing kernel.
Spring 2019 Differential Privacy And Network Analysis
This is mainly a collection of readings that I went through with my friend Kori Khan at OSU. We went through the first few readings but I later shifted my interest onto the high probability aspect of the differential privacy theory and the probability on graph and networks. This is a very nice group of readings I had in appled probability in modern datasets.
Autumn 2018 Topology Of Quantum Fields
This is mainly a collection of readings that I went through during the process of reading/revision of Prof.Talagrand's manuscript about Quantumn Field book. It is also kind of a practical ground for the algebraic skills learned in previous seminars. The depth here is not as much as a physict would expect but I personally like the subject a lot.
Summer 2018 Lie Algebras, Symmetries And Statistical Mechanics
This discussion is mainly about finite dimensional complex Lie algebras and root classification. In this series of discussions, we talked about the general concept of symmetries associated with a state space and the invariant estimation theory. In connection of the previous seminar, we also emphasized physical interpretation. This is also a prelude to the next series of seminars, about the Topology of Quantum Fields.
Spring 2018 Random Matrices
This discussion is mainly about random matrices with a perspective towards physics. In this series of discussions, we talked about the development of methodologies in random matrices and its connection to high dimensional statistics. As a preparation towards to statistical quantum mechanics, we also emphasize the application of random matrices on atomic spectra.
Autumn 2017* Topological Data Analysis
This discussion is mainly about topological data analysis as well as the conformal prediction with Prof.MacEachern and Prof.Peruggia. In this series of discussions, we talked about the development of methodologies in TDA and a close-up TDA analysis with mainly Vietoris-Rips/witness complex. We also planned to discuss the conformal prediction methodologies and transductions.
Summer ​2017* Stochastic Processes And Dynamics
This seminar was planned to be part of Prof.Peruggia's independent study course. However, due to time constraint, I only prepared the materials yet not able to bring it to discussion. The set of notes contains the basic properties of stochastic processes and two major viewpoints of treating it as random functions or dynamic system. The first viewpoint ends up with stochastic geometry and works of Dr.Adler; the second viewpoint ends up with the proof of Bernoulli conjecture and related techniques.
Spring 2017 Bayesian Robust Statistics
This is the "Five lectures in robust statistics" I learned myself at OSU, it first reviewed classical results and framework in robust statistics like Huber's M-estimator and relevant results from Banach space theory and convex analysis. I had a plan to extend the material I collected in this course into a full-length course material.
Autumn 2016* Bayesian Nonparametrics
This is an independent study course/seminar I did with Prof.S.MacEachern at OSU, it turned out to be very productive and I finally managed to organize the materials we discussed as well as recent developments like variants of Bayes nonparametric models (Dirichlet and its extensions/Gaussian models) into the monograph A Course in Bayesian Nonparametrics. It turned out to be a very beneficial course and a journey that I benefited a lot from. Later I grew my interest in Bayes nonparametrics and this is the seed.
Summer 2016* Statistical shape analysis
This is an independent study course I did with Prof.S.Kurtek at OSU, it covered some interesting models and some classical results in the analysis of directional and shape data like landmark methods and manifold frameworks. I learned a lot of literature as well as applications in the field. Part of the course is modeling a classification task suggested by Prof.A.Dena.
Winter 2015* High dimensional statistics
This is a workshop I carried out with my friends during my first semester at OSU, it basically discussed some results and techniques used frequently in high dimensional statistics. At that time my focus was obviously the mathematical nature of the subject and it did arouse my interest, I think my interest in high dimensional inference and modeling are partly from here.
Spring 2015* Representation theory
This is a workshop I participated at SYSU, it is basically based on the lectures given by Prof. Marc Rosso from Institut de Mathématiques de Jussieuopen. These lectures cover basic knowledge about the representations for classical groups as well as some recent results and techniques that help to simplify the statements and proofs.
